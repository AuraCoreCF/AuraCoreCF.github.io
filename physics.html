Physics Layer

Coherence as a physical organizing principle.

The physics side of AuraCoreCF provides the deepest theoretical foundation for the broader project. It presents coherence not merely as a descriptive analogy, but as a principle that may govern how structure, stability, and observable behavior emerge across different kinds of systems.

Central claim

The core argument is that decoherence may not be fully captured by a context-free picture. Instead, apparatus-dependent structure can matter. On this view, the organization of the measuring situation is not passive background. It is part of the conditions that shape what becomes stable, what collapses, and what remains observable.

Why this matters here

AuraCoreCF uses that same intuition as a bridge into cognition. If structure matters physically, then structure may also matter computationally in a stronger way than the standard output-only framing allows. Coherence becomes the conceptual thread linking physics, cognition, and governance.

Interpretive path

01

Apparatus dependence

The measurement context is treated as an active part of the explanatory structure rather than neutral scenery.

02

Coherence constraints

Observable stability is read through the lens of coherence and structural compatibility, not only through abstract state evolution.

03

Cross-domain implication

The same principle motivates a cognitive architecture where stable organization matters more than isolated model outputs.

Physics page purpose

This page gives technical readers the entry point they need.

A reader arriving from arXiv, Zenodo, X, or direct outreach should be able to understand the physical thesis in a few minutes: what is being proposed, why it is falsifiable, how it departs from standard assumptions, and why it matters for the larger coherence program.
